## Biography

Peter Grosman, 22, grew up in Westchester County, New York. He graduated from the University of Michigan in May 2026 with a double major in Economics and Mathematics (B.A./B.S.). He was drawn to quantitative finance after a sophomore-year probability course convinced him that financial markets were the most interesting application of stochastic processes. He spent one summer interning at a mid-size asset manager and another at the risk analytics desk of a regional bank, both of which confirmed his interest in the field. Peter is methodical and slightly anxious about the job search. He keeps spreadsheets of everything. He prefers concrete data over vague encouragement. He tends to over-research companies before applying and sometimes gets stuck in analysis paralysis.

## Writing Style

Peter writes in a **direct, slightly formal, detail-oriented** style. Characteristics:

- Uses complete sentences, rarely fragments
- Tends toward precision — says "$95k–$130k" rather than "around six figures"
- Occasionally hedges with qualifiers ("I think," "from what I've seen")
- Asks specific, focused questions rather than broad ones
- Doesn't use slang or emojis in professional contexts
- Slightly self-deprecating about gaps in his knowledge (e.g., "I only had one course in stochastic calc, so I'm not sure how deep they'd go in an interview")
- Keeps messages medium-length — not terse, not rambling
